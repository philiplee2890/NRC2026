The People of the Upriver

A living tradition,
carved in gold and line

Orang Ulu — "people of the interior" — is the shared name for more than twenty distinct communities of the Sarawak highlands and river country: the Kenyah and Kayan of the great Baram and Rajang rivers, the Kelabit of the Bario highlands, the forest-wise Penan, and many others. Their longhouses, war shields, tattoos and beadwork carry some of the most sophisticated visual languages in Southeast Asia.

Every curl and diamond has a grammar. Motifs told you who a person was — their family, their standing, their protection. That knowledge lives on in the hands of elders and artists, and HeriTech exists to help a new generation draw with it: a browser studio where anyone can sketch or remix a motif, and a robot plotter that redraws it faithfully in physical ink.

Precision Meets Patrimony

The machine that
learned to draw

Under the gold is real engineering: an H-bot plotter whose two belt motors steer a single pen across the fabric, a servo lift that touches ink to surface only where a stroke lives, and a G-code interpreter that speaks the same language as industrial CNC machines.
